## Page 2 — The 4 Findings

**Headline (top):**
> Four observations Bright's current agency hasn't reported.

**Layout: 2×2 grid of findings.**

### Finding 1 (top-left)
**Title:** CPM burn day 3 of 10
**Body:** Bright has 10 active Meta ads — all started 8 May 2026, all sharing one creative (Eid sheep on cream). At day 3 of identical-creative repetition, Meta's auction typically raises CPM 30-60% by day 10. By Eid week (27 May), the cost-per-inquiry will be roughly double what it was on launch day. The agency producing these ads bills on ad count, not outcome.

### Finding 2 (top-right)
**Title:** Organic gap = 4.6× behind Almoosa
**Body:** @bright.alahsa has 3,478 organic followers. Almoosa Aesthetic Center has 16,000. Kayan has 8,400. Eva is national. Even after 24 months of paid ad spend, the organic foundation Bright pays to reach is the foundation competitors built first. The agency cannot fix organic — that's a strategy and content problem, not an ad-budget problem.

### Finding 3 (bottom-left)
**Title:** Credentials invisible publicly
**Body:** Dr. Hassan Nazzal holds Fellowship of the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland. Dr. Marina Naddaf has been an American Academy of Dermatology member continuously since 2004. Neither credential appears on any current Bright property — not the .net website, not the .sa, not any branch handle. In an Eastern Province where Almoosa leads on institutional brand, your strongest single asset (verifiable individual credentials) is hidden.

### Finding 4 (bottom-right)
**Title:** Two domains return errors
**Body:** brightclinics.sa returns a TLS certificate error in every browser. brightclinics.net returns rate-limited HTTP 503 from Wix. Every Saudi female buyer who Googles "Bright Clinics" and lands on either of these properties sees an "unsafe" or "site down" warning. This is a trust-killing first impression for a premium clinic.
